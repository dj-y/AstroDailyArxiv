## Paper 1

**Chinese Title**:  
VLT/CRIRES+对温暖海王星WASP-107 b的观测：利用地基透射光谱探测较冷且多云系外行星分子所面临的挑战

**English Title**:  
VLT/CRIRES+ observations of warm Neptune WASP-107 b: Challenges in detecting molecules with ground-based transmission spectroscopy of cooler and cloudy exoplanets

**Chinese Abstract**:  
凌星系外行星的大气可以通过空间或地基观测进行光谱研究。两种方式各有优缺点，因此结合使用具有明显优势。对于较冷、较小且其大气很可能含有多种分子物种和云层的系外行星这类具有挑战性的目标而言，这一点尤为如此。

我们的目标是研究温暖的类海王星系外行星WASP-107 b（Teq约为740 K）的大气。近期基于JWST的空间观测研究已在这颗系外行星中探测到多种分子物种，我们希望利用地基VLT观测对这些探测结果进行确认和扩展，并评估我们的结果与此前反演得到的大气参数之间的一致性。

我们利用VLT/CRIRES+观测了WASP-107 b的两次凌星，并基于JWST研究中的反演结果构建了目标大气的互相关模板。我们制作了不同的模板，以考察不同物种体积混合比变化以及是否包含云层所带来的影响。考虑到该目标在观测上的挑战性，我们在分析真实数据之前先构建了模拟观测，以评估预期的探测显著性。

我们报告探测到了两种分子物种：CO（约6倍信噪比）和H2O（约4.5倍信噪比）。这证实了此前基于空间观测的探测结果，并首次表明VLT/CRIRES+具备通过透射光谱在比热木星更冷的目标中探测分子物种的能力。我们显示，我们的分析对是否包含云层较为敏感，但对不同体积混合比的敏感性较低。有趣的是，我们的探测在Kp-vsys图中的位置偏离了预期位置，我们也推测了其中可能的原因。我们表明，相较于更热的系外行星，较冷系外行星的误差预算会显著缩小，并强调在高分辨率光谱研究背景下仍需开展进一步工作。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2508.18964v2 Announce Type: replace
Abstract: Atmospheres of transiting exoplanets can be studied spectroscopically using space-based or ground-based observations. Each has its own strengths and weaknesses, so there are benefits to both approaches. This is especially true for challenging targets such as cooler, smaller exoplanets whose atmospheres likely contain many molecular species and cloud decks.
We aim to study the atmosphere of the warm Neptune-like exoplanet WASP-107 b (Teq~740 K). Several molecular species have been detected in this exoplanet in recent space-based JWST studies, and we aim to confirm and expand upon these detections using ground-based VLT, evaluating how well our findings agree with previously retrieved atmospheric parameters.
We observe two transits of WASP-107 b with VLT/CRIRES+ and create cross-correlation templates of the target atmosphere based on retrieval results from JWST studies. We create different templates to investigate the impact of varying volume mixing ratios of species and inclusion or exclusion of clouds. Considering this target's observational challenges, we create simulated observations prior to evaluating real data to assess expected detection significances.
We report detections of two molecular species, CO (~6 S/N) and H2O (~4.5 S/N). This confirms previous space-based detections and demonstrates, for the first time, the capability of VLT/CRIRES+ to detect species in targets cooler than hot Jupiters using transmission spectroscopy. We show our analysis is sensitive to cloud inclusion, but less so to different volume mixing ratios. Interestingly, our detection deviates from its expected location in our Kp-vsys diagrams, and we speculate on possible reasons for this. We demonstrate that the error budget for relatively cooler exoplanets is severely reduced in comparison to hotter exoplanets, and underline need for further work in context of high-resolution spectroscopy.
