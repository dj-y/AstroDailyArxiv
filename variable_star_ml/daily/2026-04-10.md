## Paper 1

**Chinese Title**:  
ASTRAFier：一种新颖且可扩展的基于 Transformer 的恒星变异性分类器

**English Title**:  
ASTRAFier: A Novel and Scalable Transformer-based Stellar Variability Classifier

**Chinese Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.07437v1 公告类型：新稿
摘要：Kepler 和 TESS 等测光任务已生成数百万条光变曲线，几乎覆盖整个天空，为研究恒星变异性并推进我们对宇宙的理解提供了前所未有的机遇。在这一数据极其丰富的环境中，机器学习已成为一种强有力的工具，能够高效且准确地根据恒星变异类型对光变曲线进行处理和分类。在这项工作中，我们提出了 ASTRAFier：一种新颖的基于 Transformer 的变异性分类模型，该模型融合了双向长短期记忆网络（BiLSTM）和卷积神经网络（CNN）。该模型可直接作用于时间序列，无需进行特征工程，从而构建了一个易于维护且高效的端到端分类框架。我们使用 Kepler 和 TESS 的光变曲线对模型进行了训练和验证，分别在 Kepler 上取得了 $94.26\%$ 的分类准确率，在 TESS 上取得了 $88.22\%$ 的分类准确率。我们通过将模型部署到由 MIT 快速预览流水线（QLP）提供的、来自第 14、15 和 26 扇区（Kepler 视场）的约 $\sim 2.8$ 百万条 TESS 光变曲线上，展示了其可扩展性，并发布了由此得到的恒星变异性目录。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.07437v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: Photometric missions such as Kepler and TESS have generated millions of light curves covering almost the entire sky, offering unprecedented opportunities to study stellar variability and advance our understanding of the Universe. In this data-rich environment, machine learning has emerged as a powerful tool to efficiently and accurately process and classify light curves according to their type of stellar variability. In this work, we introduce ASTRAFier: a novel Transformer-based model for variability classification that integrates Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory (BiLSTM) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). The model operates directly on time series without requiring feature engineering, creating an easy-to-maintain and efficient end-to-end classification framework. We train and validate our model using both Kepler and TESS light curves and, respectively, achieve a classification accuracy of $94.26\%$ on Kepler and $88.22\%$ on TESS. We demonstrate scalability by deploying our model on $\sim 2.8$ million TESS light curves from sectors 14, 15, and 26 (Kepler Field-of-View) delivered by MIT's Quick-look Pipeline (QLP) and release the resulting stellar variability catalog.

**Authors**:  
Paul F. X. Gregory, Jeroen Audenaert, Mykyta Kliapets, Daniel Muthukrishna, Andrew Tkachenko, Marek Skarka, George R. Ricker

## Paper 2

**Chinese Title**:  
长次级周期的谐波相位诊断：在 OGLE 样本中检验振荡对流偶极模的预测

**English Title**:  
Harmonic phase diagnostics of long secondary periods. Testing predictions of oscillatory convective dipole modes in the OGLE sample

**Chinese Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.06563v2 公告类型：替换
摘要：高光度红巨星中的长次级周期（LSP）仍然是长周期恒星变异现象中唯一尚无可靠物理起源解释的主要类别。相互竞争的假说包括具有尘埃伴星的双星系统，以及振荡对流偶极模。我们识别出在何种物理与几何条件下，振荡对流偶极模会产生有别于双星系统预期信号的独特谐波特征，并将这一诊断方法应用于经过筛选的 OGLE-III LSP 样本子集，以寻找与振荡对流偶极模一致的实例。我们对振荡对流偶极模引起的几何通量调制进行了建模，并描绘了在何种倾角、温度振幅和观测波长范围内，谐波特征可以被观测到。利用 OGLE-III 的 I 波段光变曲线，我们要求在序列 D 及其谐波上都具有统计显著的功率，从而保留了一个包含 249 颗恒星的筛选样本（占按脊线选取样本的 2.1%）。我们采用迭代的 Lomb-Scargle 方法和加权傅里叶分解来分离基频与谐波成分。两者之间的相对相位（$\Delta\phi$）能够区分：由倾斜偶极子预测的次峰，以及由食双星或椭球双星系统造成的次谷。在该筛选子集中，大多数高振幅恒星表现出的 $\Delta\phi$ 与双星系统产生的次谷相一致。然而，其中有一个虽小但在统计上不可忽略的子集，其 $\Delta\phi$ 与次峰相一致，这种现象难以用食双星或椭球双星来解释，反而与高倾角、非旋转的振荡对流偶极模的几何预测相符，其温度振幅也与已发表模型一致。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.06563v2 Announce Type: replace
Abstract: Long secondary periods (LSPs) in luminous red giants remain the only major class of long-period stellar variability without a secure physical origin. Competing hypotheses include binaries with dusty companions and oscillatory convective dipole modes. We identify the physical and geometric conditions under which oscillatory convective dipole modes produce distinctive harmonic signatures that contrast with those expected from binary systems, and apply this diagnostic to a filtered subset of the OGLE-III LSP sample to identify examples consistent with oscillatory convective dipole modes. We model the geometric flux modulation from oscillatory convective dipole modes and map the range of inclinations, temperature amplitudes, and observing wavelengths for which harmonic features are observable. Using OGLE-III I-band light curves, we require statistically significant power at both sequence D and its harmonic, keeping a filtered sample of 249 stars (2.1% of the ridge-selected sample). We apply iterative Lomb-Scargle and weighted Fourier decomposition to isolate the fundamental and harmonic components. The relative phase ($\Delta\phi$) between these distinguishes secondary maxima predicted by an inclined dipole from secondary minima caused by eclipsing or ellipsoidal binary systems. The majority of high amplitude stars in the filtered subset show $\Delta\phi$ consistent with secondary minima produced by binary systems. However, a small but statistically non-negligible subset exhibits $\Delta\phi$ consistent with secondary maxima that are difficult to reconcile by eclipsing or ellipsoidal binaries, and instead match the geometric predictions for highly inclined, non-rotating oscillatory convective dipole modes with temperature amplitudes consistent with published models.

**Authors**:  
Benjamin Courtney-Barrer, Xavier Haubois, Michael Ireland, Peter Wood
