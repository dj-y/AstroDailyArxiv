StarCLR：用于天文光变曲线的对比学习表征框架

StarCLR: Contrastive Learning Representation for Astronomical Light Curves

**Chinese Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.24516v1 公告类型：new
摘要：随着时域巡天的快速发展，海量光变曲线数据的可获得性为研究恒星演化和变星分类带来了新的机遇，同时也对特征提取与建模提出了挑战。我们提出 StarCLR，一个面向大规模光变曲线的对比式预训练框架。通过从部分重叠的子序列中构造正样本对，StarCLR 促使模型学习时间表征。我们在 TESS 数据集上对 StarCLR 进行预训练，并将其微调用于三个具有不同观测特性的巡天中的变星分类，即 TESS（18 类）、ZTF（11 类）和 Gaia（24 类）。StarCLR 分别取得了 84.35%、87.82% 和 92.73% 的宏平均 F1 分数，以及 94.46%、92.83% 和 99.49% 的微平均 F1 分数。与从头训练的 LSTM 和 Transformer 相比，StarCLR 在 TESS 和 ZTF 上表现更优，其中在采样稀疏的 ZTF 光变曲线上提升最大，展现出良好的泛化能力。对于涉及更大类别空间的 Gaia，由于评估方式无法直接比较，且性能可能受到天体物理特征的影响，预训练骨干网络的贡献因此相对有限。针对嵌入设计、池化策略和预训练设置的系统性消融实验进一步表明，预训练表征能够通过捕捉光变曲线中富有信息的时间特征来带来性能提升。展望未来，随着数据集标准化以及更加多样的标注方案的建立，StarCLR 的泛化能力还有望进一步增强。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.24516v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: With the rapid development of time-domain surveys, the availability of massive light curve data offers new opportunities for studying stellar evolution and variable star classification, while simultaneously posing challenges for feature extraction and modeling. We present StarCLR, a contrastive pretraining framework for large-scale light curves. By constructing positive pairs from partially overlapping sub-sequences, StarCLR encourages the model to learn temporal representations. We pretrain StarCLR on the TESS dataset and fine-tune it for variable star classification on three surveys with distinct observational characteristics, namely TESS (18 types), ZTF (11 types), and Gaia (24 types). StarCLR achieves macro-F1 scores of 84.35%, 87.82%, and 92.73%, and micro-F1 scores of 94.46%, 92.83%, and 99.49%, respectively. Compared with LSTM and Transformer trained from scratch, StarCLR performs better on TESS and ZTF, with the largest gains on sparsely sampled ZTF light curves, demonstrating promising generalization. For Gaia, which involves a broader class space, the evaluation is not directly comparable, and performance is likely influenced by astrophysical features, resulting in a more limited contribution from the pretrained backbone. Systematic ablations on embedding design, pooling strategy, and pretraining settings further indicate that the pretrained representations provide performance gains by capturing informative temporal characteristics of light curves. Looking ahead, with standardized datasets and more diverse labeling schemes, the generalization ability of StarCLR can be further enhanced.

Abstract: Light curves of oscillating stars provide valuable insights into the stellar interiors. When oscillations from a pair of stars are captured within a single photometric aperture, they can be considered as potential asteroseismic binaries (ABs). We highlight the importance of identifying and accurately accounting for such systems in asteroseismic analysis.

摘要：我们分析了 767 颗 Gaia 径向速度（RV）标准星的径向速度测量及磁活动指标（S 指数和 BIS）中的周期性，以区分恒星活动信号与行星信号。在这些恒星中，仅有 359 颗检测到显著的 RV 周期。我们通过迭代周期图分析识别出自转周期和磁活动周期。在已确认存在行星的恒星中，$28.2\%$ 的 RV 信号与活动指标重合，而无行星恒星中这一比例为 $21.3\%$；然而，统计检验表明这一差异在统计上并不显著。此前曾归因于行星的若干 RV 信号，例如 HIP7240、HIP28460 和 HIP48331 中的信号，更可能是由恒星活动引起，这强调了使用多种诊断手段评估 RV 变异性的重要性。我们给出了 125 颗恒星的自转周期，其中 30 个为新估计值，并在 127 颗恒星中探测到磁活动周期，其中 95 个为新发现。我们还在恒星发电机理论框架下进一步研究了自转周期与磁活动周期之间的关系。完整样本在 $P_{\rm rot}/P_{\rm cyc}$ 与罗斯贝数图上呈现连续分布，缺乏经典的活动支与非活动支划分，反而显示出负斜率，这与一些早期研究相反。有趣的是，带有行星伴星的恒星表现出比非宿主恒星更陡的趋势（斜率分别为 $-1.049 \pm 0.078$ 和 $-0.654 \pm 0.056$），这表明行星的存在可能会对宿主恒星的磁行为产生微妙影响。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.23694v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: We analyze periodicities in radial velocity (RV) measurements and magnetic activity indicators (S-index and BIS) for 767 Gaia RV standard stars to distinguish between stellar activity and planetary signals. Significant RV periods were detected in only 359 of these stars. Rotation and magnetic cycle periods are identified through iterative periodogram analysis. Among stars with confirmed planets, $28.2\%$ exhibit RV signals that coincide with activity indicators, compared to $21.3\%$ among stars without planets; however, statistical tests show this difference is not statistically significant. Several RV signals previously attributed to planets, such as those in HIP7240, HIP28460, and HIP48331, are instead likely caused by stellar activity, emphasising the importance of using multiple diagnostics to assess RV variability. We report rotation periods in 125 stars, including 30 new estimates, and detect magnetic activity cycles in 127 stars, 95 of which are new. We further investigate the relationship between rotation and magnetic cycle periods in the context of stellar dynamo theory. The full sample reveals a continuous distribution in the $P_{\rm rot}/P_{\rm cyc}$, Rossby number diagram, lacking the classical division into active and inactive branches and instead showing a negative slope, in contrast to some earlier studies. Interestingly, stars with planetary companions exhibit a steeper trend (slope of $-1.049 \pm 0.078$) compared to non-hosts ($-0.654 \pm 0.056$), suggesting that the presence of planets may subtly influence the host star's magnetic behaviour.
