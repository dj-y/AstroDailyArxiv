## Paper 1

**Chinese Title**:  
食双星的基本有效温度测量——VIII. CD-27 2812 的 NIRPS 光谱观测

**English Title**:  
Fundamental effective temperature measurements for eclipsing binary stars -- VIII. NIRPS spectroscopy of CD-27 2812

**Chinese Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.13631v1 公告类型：新投稿
摘要：目前，具有质量、半径和有效温度（T$_{\rm eff}$）的精确且彼此独立测量结果、可用于检验这类低质量恒星理论模型的 M 矮星非常少。我们的目标是利用高分辨率近红外光谱，测量与太阳型恒星构成食双星系统的 M 矮星质量，并测量两颗恒星在近红外波段的流量比。随后可将这些信息与光变曲线、测光以及视差分析结合起来，从而测定两颗恒星的质量、半径和 T$_{\rm eff}$。我们利用 TESS 光变曲线以及 HARPS 和 NIRPS 光谱仪获得的光谱，对 CD-27 2812 进行了测量。该系统中的主星是一颗 F9 V 星，与一颗暗得多的 M 矮星伴星构成食双星，后者沿一条短周期、近圆轨道运行（P=7.8 d）。我们得到了如下不依赖恒星模型的半径和质量：$R_1 = 1.721 \pm 0.004 R_{\odot}$、$R_2 = 0.531 \pm 0.002 R_{\odot}$、$M_1 = 1.3597 \pm 0.0024 M_{\odot}$，以及 $M_2 = 0.5624 \pm 0.0006 M_{\odot}$。我们展示了如何利用 NIRPS 光谱测量 J 和 H 波段中的流量比。将这些信息与已发表的测光数据及 Gaia DR3 视差结合，可得到如下有效温度测量值：$T_{\rm eff,1} = 6197 \pm 55$ K，$T_{\rm eff,2} = 3770 \pm 28$ K。本研究表明，如今已可以利用食双星系统，对同时具有独立质量和半径测量结果的 M 矮星进行高精度的 T$_{\rm eff}$ 测量。

**English Abstract**:  
arXiv:2604.13631v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: There are very few M-dwarfs with accurate independent measurements of their mass, radius and effective temperature (T$_{\rm eff}$) that can be used to test stellar models for these low-mass stars. We aim to use high-resolution, near-infrared spectroscopy to measure the mass of M-dwarfs in eclipsing binary systems with solar-type stars and to measure the flux ratio between the two stars at near-infrared wavelengths. This information can then be combined with the analysis of the light curve, photometry, and the parallax to measure the mass, radius and T$_{\rm eff}$ for both stars. We have used the TESS light curve and spectra observed with the HARPS and NIRPS spectrographs to measure the following model-independent radii and masses for CD-27 2812, an F9 V star in an eclipsing binary with a much fainter M-dwarf companion on a short near-circular orbit (P=7.8 d) : $R_1 = 1.721 \pm 0.004 R_{\odot}$, $R_2 = 0.531 \pm 0.002 R_{\odot}$, $M_1 = 1.3597 \pm 0.0024 M_{\odot}$, and $M_2 = 0.5624 \pm 0.0006 M_{\odot}$ We show how the NIRPS spectra can be used to measure the flux ratio in the J and H bands. This information, combined with published photometry and the Gaia DR3 parallax, leads to the following effective temperature measurements: $T_{\rm eff,1} = 6197 \pm 55$ K, $T_{\rm eff,2} = 3770 \pm 28$ K. This study demonstrates that it is now feasible to use eclipsing binaries to accurately measure T$_{\rm eff}$ for M-dwarf stars for which we also have independent mass and radius measurements.
